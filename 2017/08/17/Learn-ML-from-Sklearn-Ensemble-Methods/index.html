Posted on 2017-08-17
study notes for scikit-learn 3

Boosting vs. Bagging

Bagging and boosting are both ensemble methods to turn weak classifiers into a strong one.

Bagging

Bagging is to train a bunch of same weak learners from a subset of training data. The subset is taken using bootstrap method (sampling with replacement), and could be a subset of all training examples or a subset of all features.

Bagging, such as random forests achieves a lower test error solely by variance reduction. Therefore increasing the number of trees in the ensemble won’t have any effect on the bias of your model. Higher number of trees will only reduce the variance of your model. Moreover you can achieve a higher variance reduction by reducing the correlation between trees in the ensemble. This is the reason why we randomly select ‘m’ attributes at each split because it will introduce some randomness in to the ensemble and reduce the correlation between trees. Hence ‘m’ is the major attribute to be tuned in a random forest ensemble. A small value of m will reduce the variance of the ensemble but will also increase the bias of an individual tree in the ensemble.

Boosting

Boosting is to train a bunch of different weak learners from the whole set of training data. The whole idea is to iteratively add weights to examples that were wrongly classified by previous classifier combinations.

Random Forest

Random forest builds a lot of decision trees with a subset of samples and a subset of variables. These decision trees will vote to form the final classification decision. It’s a bagging method.

Parameters

  • max_features: recommended to select through cross validation. The start number could be either square root, or a certain percentage of the whole no. of features. Increasing max_features will reduce the randomness amongst trees though increase variance; but decreasing max_features might lead to bias. Finding the right balance is important.
  • n_estimators: the more the better up to 2000-10000.
  • min_samples_leaf: recommended to be more than 50. The large the number is, the more detailed are learnt from training sample.
  • Criterion: It sometimes has a small impact.
